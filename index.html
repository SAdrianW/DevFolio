About Me

I am an avid and passionate creator. I find my greatest joy in life comes from making things that either bring joy to others or that stand out as being different. The forms that my creations can take are many and varied. I have produced everything from small scale sculptures to fully painted house interiors to code based websites and games.

Having created so many various things and tried my hand at so many different disciplines I find that while I have picked up a lot of useful skills, I have yet to find my place in the world. I believe that I have found it now with Software Engineering, due to the depth and breadth of scope of potential projects that can be achieved.

SonarSweeper

Imagine yourself in command of an Allied convy escort ship during WWII.

It would be nothing like this at all, but it served as the inspiration for my very first software project.

SonarSweeper is a nautical themed take on Minesweeper that I created using JavaScript, HTML5 and CSS3. This project was the first time that I had a chance to use recursion. This project was the first time that I had a chance to use recursion.

HammerSpace

What I felt that my portfolio needed at this point was a project that was a portfolio.

As an avid collector of miniatures I have accrued quite the collection over the two decades that I have been collecting.

In order to remind myself of exactly what was in my collection I created HammerSpace. The main tools used were MongoDB, Express.js and Node.js. Honourable mentions go to Oauth2, Bootstrap and Cloudinary.

Destinations In Time

For our introduction to Python during bootcamp, we were required to make a group project. After some collaboration with my group about what we all wanted to make. It was settled on a Travel Agency with a historical theme.

Also introduced in this project were relational databases, specifically PostgreSQL. Since I had elected to spearhead the front-end design for this project, I decided to keep minimal involvement from Bootstrap (since it has some helpful features that we used) and do most of the CSS myself to get more practice in.
